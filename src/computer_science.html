Computer Science

  • BBC Bitesize (GCSE & A-Level Computer Science) - Fantastic free resource with clear notes, videos, quizzes, and activities aligned with exam boards
  • Isaac Computer Science - Superb free online textbook from University of Cambridge and Raspberry Pi Foundation with interactive questions
  • Physics & Maths Tutor Education - Excellent free revision notes, topic questions, and past papers for all exam boards
  • Seneca Learning - Free AI-powered platform using spaced repetition and active recall for effective learning
  • Save My Exams - Quality revision notes, exam-style questions, and past papers tailored to specific exam boards
  • Craig 'n' Dave (YouTube & Website) - Extremely popular comprehensive video tutorials covering entire syllabus
  • Teach Computing - Part of National Centre for Computing Education with wealth of resources for teachers and students
  • Khan Academy Computer Science - Excellent conceptual explanations for foundational computer science topics
  • Computer Science: An Overview
  • J. Glenn Brookshear
  • Comprehensive introduction to computer science concepts. Perfect for GCSE students to understand the breadth of the field.
  • The Art of Computer Programming
  • Donald E. Knuth
  • The definitive work on computer programming. Excellent for A-Level students considering further study in computer science.
  • Introduction to Algorithms
  • Thomas H. Cormen
  • Comprehensive guide to algorithms and their analysis. Great for understanding computational thinking.
  • Logic in Computer Science
  • Michael Huth
  • Excellent introduction to logic and its applications in computer science. Perfect for developing analytical skills.
  • Artificial Intelligence: A Modern Approach
  • Stuart Russell
  • Comprehensive introduction to AI concepts and techniques. Great for students interested in cutting-edge technology.
  • Superintelligence
  • Nick Bostrom
  • Explores the future of AI and its implications for society. Fascinating read for students interested in AI ethics.
  • Clean Code
  • Robert C. Martin
  • Essential guide to writing readable and maintainable code. Perfect for students learning to program.
  • The Pragmatic Programmer
  • Andrew Hunt
  • Practical advice for becoming a better programmer. Excellent for developing professional programming skills.
  • Weapons of Math Destruction
  • Cathy O'Neil
  • Explores the dark side of algorithms and their impact on society. Important reading for understanding technology's social implications.
  • The Code: Silicon Valley and the Remaking of America
  • Margaret O'Mara
  • Historical perspective on the development of Silicon Valley and its impact on society. Great for understanding the broader context of computer science.
  • Lumiere Research Scholar Program
  • Apply Here
  • This program pairs high school students with PhD mentors from top universities to conduct independent research projects. They have a specific focus on computer science and technology.
  • Polygence
  • Visit Website
  • Similar to Lumiere, Polygence connects students with academic mentors for one-on-one research projects. The platform is flexible and can accommodate a wide range of computer science topics.
  • Cambridge Centre for International Research
  • Visit Website
  • CCIR offers research mentorship programs in various subjects, including computer science. Their programs are designed to provide a university-level research experience.
  • Immerse Education Computer Science Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • Immerse offers in-person computer science programs at prestigious universities like Oxford and Cambridge. The curriculum covers programming, algorithms, and software engineering.
  • Imperial College London Global Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • This program offers specialized areas of study, including computer science and engineering, allowing students to engage in academic lectures and practical challenges.
  • Conrad Challenge
  • Visit Website
  • This is a global competition for students to create innovative solutions to global challenges. A team could develop a computer science-related project to address software or AI problems.
  • International Olympiad in Informatics
  • Visit Website
  • This is the most prestigious computer science competition for high school students globally. To participate, students must typically first succeed in their national informatics olympiad.
  • Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair
  • Visit Website
  • ISEF is the world's largest pre-college STEM competition. Students qualify through local and regional fairs, and the event offers significant awards and networking opportunities.
  • British Informatics Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This competition recognizes outstanding high school students for their computer science knowledge and problem-solving skills. It offers recognition and opportunities for further competition.
  • Online Courses
  • Many universities and platforms offer free or low-cost online courses (MOOCs) in computer science and related fields. Platforms like Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn have courses from top institutions.
  • Science Communication
  • Look for opportunities to write for a school science magazine, start a blog, or create a YouTube channel about computer science topics. This is a great way to deepen your understanding.
  • Local Organizations
  • Check with local technology companies, software firms, universities, or coding bootcamps for volunteer opportunities. Many of these places have programs for young people.
