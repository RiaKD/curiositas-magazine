Chemistry

  • BBC Bitesize (GCSE & A-Level Chemistry) - Superb free resource with interactive notes, videos, quizzes, and activities
  • Chemistry & Maths Tutor Education - Essential free resource with extensive revision notes, past papers, and mark schemes
  • Seneca Learning - Free engaging platform using neuroscience-backed learning methods with GIFs and animations
  • Save My Exams - High-quality revision notes, exam-style questions, and past papers for all exam boards
  • A-Level Chemistry Online - Comprehensive free video tutorials covering entire AQA specification
  • Isaac Chemistry - Free platform from University of Cambridge with challenging chemistry problems
  • Hyperchemistry - Fantastic in-depth resource for understanding chemistry concepts
  • Khan Academy Chemistry - Wide range of free video lessons and practice exercises for conceptual understanding
  • A Short History of Nearly Everything
  • Bill Bryson
  • While not exclusively chemistry, this book offers an incredibly engaging and accessible journey through scientific discoveries, including many chemical ones, in a way that makes complex concepts understandable and fun. Highly recommended for both GCSE and A-Level.
  • Molecules: The Elements and the Architecture of Everything
  • Theodore Gray
  • A beautifully illustrated book that explores the molecules that make up our world, providing a visual and engaging introduction to chemical structures and their properties. Great for all levels.
  • The Disappearing Spoon: And Other True Tales of Madness, Love, and the History of the World from the Periodic Table of the Elements
  • Sam Kean
  • Explores the fascinating stories, history, and bizarre facts behind each element on the periodic table. Very entertaining and informative.
  • Uncle Tungsten: Memories of a Chemical Boyhood
  • Oliver Sacks
  • A memoir by the renowned neurologist, recounting his childhood fascination with chemistry and the scientific process. Offers a personal and inspiring perspective on the subject.
  • Napoleon's Buttons: How 17 Molecules Changed History
  • Penny Le Couteur and Jay Burreson
  • Explores how specific molecules have had a profound impact on historical events, from disease to war to fashion. A great way to see chemistry's real-world relevance.
  • Stuff Matters: Exploring the Marvelous Materials That Shape Our Man-Made World
  • Mark Miodownik
  • Explores the science behind everyday materials like paper, concrete, chocolate, and steel. Highly engaging and shows how chemistry underpins material science.
  • Salt: A World History
  • Mark Kurlansky
  • A comprehensive history of salt, detailing its significance in human civilization, trade, and even chemical processes.
  • The Case of the Poisoner's Ring / The Case of the Deadly Dandelion (and others in The Elements of Murder series)
  • John Emsley
  • These books use real-life cases of poisoning and crime to illustrate chemical principles in a gripping, detective-story style.
  • The Poisoner's Handbook: Murder and the Birth of Forensic Medicine in Jazz Age New York
  • Deborah Blum
  • A fascinating look at the early days of forensic chemistry in the US, exploring how chemists developed methods to detect poisons and solve crimes.
  • Oxygen: The Molecule that Made the World
  • Nick Lane
  • Explores the crucial role of oxygen in the evolution of life and its fundamental chemical properties.
  • Mendeleyev's Dream: The Quest For The Elements
  • Paul Strathern
  • A captivating narrative about the history of the discovery of the elements and the creation of the periodic table.
  • Bad Science
  • Ben Goldacre
  • While not exclusively chemistry, this book is vital for developing critical thinking skills by exposing scientific misinformation, including that related to chemicals and health claims. Essential for A-Level students.
  • The Periodic Table
  • Primo Levi
  • A unique and profound book where each chapter is named after a chemical element and serves as a metaphor for an episode in the author's life, particularly his experiences during the Holocaust. Offers a literary and philosophical perspective on chemistry.
  • Atkins' Molecules
  • P.W. Atkins
  • A concise and elegant introduction to molecular structure and bonding from a renowned chemist. More theoretical, suitable for strong A-Level students.
  • Chemistry3: Introducing Inorganic, Organic and Physical Chemistry
  • Andrew Burrows et al.
  • While a textbook, dipping into chapters of a university-level introductory text can provide a more in-depth understanding of A-Level topics and beyond. Don't feel pressured to read it cover-to-cover, but use it for specific areas of interest.
  • Lumiere Research Scholar Program
  • Apply Here
  • This program pairs high school students with PhD mentors from top universities to conduct independent research projects. They have a specific focus on chemistry and engineering.
  • Polygence
  • Visit Website
  • Similar to Lumiere, Polygence connects students with academic mentors for one-on-one research projects. The platform is flexible and can accommodate a wide range of chemistry topics.
  • Cambridge Centre for International Research
  • Visit Website
  • CCIR offers research mentorship programs in various subjects, including chemistry. Their programs are designed to provide a university-level research experience.
  • Immerse Education Chemistry Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • Immerse offers in-person chemistry programs at prestigious universities like Oxford and Cambridge. The curriculum covers mechanics, thermodynamics, and modern chemistry.
  • Imperial College London Global Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • This program offers specialized areas of study, including chemistry and engineering, allowing students to engage in academic lectures and practical challenges.
  • Conrad Challenge
  • Visit Website
  • This is a global competition for students to create innovative solutions to global challenges. A team could develop a chemistry-related project to address energy or transportation issues.
  • International Chemistry Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This is the most prestigious chemistry competition for high school students globally. To participate, students must typically first succeed in their national chemistry olympiad.
  • Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair
  • Visit Website
  • ISEF is the world's largest pre-college STEM competition. Students qualify through local and regional fairs, and the event offers significant awards and networking opportunities.
  • British Chemistry Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This competition recognizes outstanding high school students for their chemistry knowledge and problem-solving skills. It offers recognition and opportunities for further competition.
  • Online Courses
  • Many universities and platforms offer free or low-cost online courses (MOOCs) in chemistry and related fields. Platforms like Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn have courses from top institutions.
  • Science Communication
  • Look for opportunities to write for a school science magazine, start a blog, or create a YouTube channel about chemistry topics. This is a great way to deepen your understanding.
  • Local Organizations
  • Check with local science museums, planetariums, engineering companies, or research institutions for volunteer opportunities. Many of these places have programs for young people.
