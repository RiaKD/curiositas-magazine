Chemistry

  • BBC Bitesize (GCSE & A-Level Chemistry) - Superb free resource with interactive notes, videos, quizzes, and activities
  • Chemistry & Maths Tutor Education - Essential free resource with extensive revision notes, past papers, and mark schemes
  • Seneca Learning - Free engaging platform using neuroscience-backed learning methods with GIFs and animations
  • Save My Exams - High-quality revision notes, exam-style questions, and past papers for all exam boards
  • A-Level Chemistry Online - Comprehensive free video tutorials covering entire AQA specification
  • Isaac Chemistry - Free platform from University of Cambridge with challenging chemistry problems
  • Hyperchemistry - Fantastic in-depth resource for understanding chemistry concepts
  • Khan Academy Chemistry - Wide range of free video lessons and practice exercises for conceptual understanding
  • The Chemistry Book
  • Clifford A. Pickover
  • A comprehensive journey through 250 milestones in chemistry history. Perfect for GCSE students to understand the development of chemistry concepts.
  • Chemistry for Scientists and Engineers
  • Raymond A. Serway
  • A clear and comprehensive textbook that covers all fundamental chemistry concepts. Excellent for A-Level students.
  • Classical Mechanics
  • Herbert Goldstein
  • The definitive guide to classical mechanics. Great for students interested in understanding motion and forces in detail.
  • Mechanics
  • Keith R. Symon
  • A clear introduction to mechanics with excellent examples and problem-solving approaches.
  • Quantum Chemistry for Beginners
  • Zbigniew Ficek
  • An accessible introduction to quantum chemistry concepts. Perfect for A-Level students exploring modern chemistry.
  • The Feynman Lectures on Chemistry
  • Richard P. Feynman
  • Classic lectures that provide deep insights into chemistry concepts. Highly recommended for advanced students.
  • Astrochemistry for People in a Hurry
  • Neil deGrasse Tyson
  • A concise and engaging introduction to astrochemistry concepts. Great for students interested in space and cosmology.
  • An Introduction to Modern Astrochemistry
  • Bradley W. Carroll
  • Comprehensive coverage of astrochemistry topics. Excellent for students considering further study in this field.
  • Thinking Chemistry
  • Lewis Carroll Epstein
  • Uses thought experiments to develop critical thinking skills in chemistry. Perfect for developing problem-solving abilities.
  • Lumiere Research Scholar Program
  • Apply Here
  • This program pairs high school students with PhD mentors from top universities to conduct independent research projects. They have a specific focus on chemistry and engineering.
  • Polygence
  • Visit Website
  • Similar to Lumiere, Polygence connects students with academic mentors for one-on-one research projects. The platform is flexible and can accommodate a wide range of chemistry topics.
  • Cambridge Centre for International Research
  • Visit Website
  • CCIR offers research mentorship programs in various subjects, including chemistry. Their programs are designed to provide a university-level research experience.
  • Immerse Education Chemistry Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • Immerse offers in-person chemistry programs at prestigious universities like Oxford and Cambridge. The curriculum covers mechanics, thermodynamics, and modern chemistry.
  • Imperial College London Global Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • This program offers specialized areas of study, including chemistry and engineering, allowing students to engage in academic lectures and practical challenges.
  • Conrad Challenge
  • Visit Website
  • This is a global competition for students to create innovative solutions to global challenges. A team could develop a chemistry-related project to address energy or transportation issues.
  • International Chemistry Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This is the most prestigious chemistry competition for high school students globally. To participate, students must typically first succeed in their national chemistry olympiad.
  • Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair
  • Visit Website
  • ISEF is the world's largest pre-college STEM competition. Students qualify through local and regional fairs, and the event offers significant awards and networking opportunities.
  • British Chemistry Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This competition recognizes outstanding high school students for their chemistry knowledge and problem-solving skills. It offers recognition and opportunities for further competition.
  • Online Courses
  • Many universities and platforms offer free or low-cost online courses (MOOCs) in chemistry and related fields. Platforms like Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn have courses from top institutions.
  • Science Communication
  • Look for opportunities to write for a school science magazine, start a blog, or create a YouTube channel about chemistry topics. This is a great way to deepen your understanding.
  • Local Organizations
  • Check with local science museums, planetariums, engineering companies, or research institutions for volunteer opportunities. Many of these places have programs for young people.
