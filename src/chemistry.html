Chemistry

  • BBC Bitesize (GCSE & A-Level Chemistry) - Free resource with notes, interactive activities, videos, and quizzes
  • Physics & Maths Tutor Education - Goldmine of free resources with detailed revision notes and past papers
  • Seneca Learning - Free interactive courses using smart algorithms to help learning and retention
  • Chemguide - Incredibly detailed website for understanding complex chemical concepts and mechanisms
  • Save My Exams - High-quality revision notes, exam-style questions, and past papers for all exam boards
  • CK Chemistry - Free downloadable revision resources including multiple-choice questions
  • A-level Chemistry.co.uk - Revision guides, quizzes, mind maps, and practice exam booklets
  • Cognito - Video lessons, notes, practice questions, and flashcards for GCSE and A-Level
  • The Disappearing Spoon
  • Sam Kean
  • A fascinating journey through the periodic table, telling the stories of the elements and the scientists who discovered them. Perfect for GCSE students to understand the human side of chemistry.
  • Chemistry: The Central Science
  • Theodore L. Brown
  • A comprehensive textbook that covers all fundamental concepts clearly. Excellent for A-Level students who want to deepen their understanding.
  • The Chemistry of Life
  • Steven Rose
  • Explores the chemical processes that make life possible. Great for students interested in biochemistry and the chemistry of living systems.
  • Chemistry in Context
  • Graham Hill
  • Shows how chemistry applies to everyday life and modern technology. Perfect for understanding the practical applications of chemistry.
  • Why Chemical Reactions Happen
  • James Keeler
  • Delves into the fundamental principles that drive chemical reactions. Excellent for A-Level students wanting to understand the 'why' behind chemistry.
  • Chemical Structure and Reactivity
  • James Keeler
  • Explores how molecular structure determines chemical behavior. Great for understanding the relationship between structure and function.
  • Physical Chemistry
  • Peter Atkins
  • A comprehensive guide to physical chemistry principles. Essential reading for students considering further study in chemistry.
  • Organic Chemistry
  • Jonathan Clayden
  • The definitive guide to organic chemistry. Highly recommended for students interested in organic chemistry and its applications.
    • Lumiere Research Scholar Program
    • Apply Here
    • This program pairs high school students with PhD mentors from top universities to conduct independent research projects. They have a specific focus on chemistry and materials science.
    • Polygence
    • Visit Website
    • Similar to Lumiere, Polygence connects students with academic mentors for one-on-one research projects. The platform is flexible and can accommodate a wide range of chemistry topics.
    • Cambridge Centre for International Research
    • Visit Website
    • CCIR offers research mentorship programs in various subjects, including chemistry. Their programs are designed to provide a university-level research experience.
    • Immerse Education Chemistry Summer School
    • Visit Website
    • Immerse offers in-person chemistry programs at prestigious universities like Oxford and Cambridge. The curriculum covers organic chemistry, inorganic chemistry, and physical chemistry.
    • Imperial College London Global Summer School
    • Visit Website
    • This program offers specialized areas of study, including chemistry and materials science, allowing students to engage in academic lectures and practical challenges.
    • Conrad Challenge
    • Visit Website
    • This is a global competition for students to create innovative solutions to global challenges. A team could develop a chemistry-related project to address environmental issues.
    • International Chemistry Olympiad
    • Visit Website
    • This is the most prestigious chemistry competition for high school students globally. To participate, students must typically first succeed in their national chemistry olympiad.
    • Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair
    • Visit Website
    • ISEF is the world's largest pre-college STEM competition. Students qualify through local and regional fairs, and the event offers significant awards and networking opportunities.
    • Chemistry World Chemistry Olympiad
    • Visit Website
    • This competition recognizes outstanding high school students for their chemistry knowledge and problem-solving skills. It offers cash prizes and recognition.
    • Online Courses
    • Many universities and platforms offer free or low-cost online courses (MOOCs) in chemistry and related fields. Platforms like Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn have courses from top institutions.
    • Science Communication
    • Look for opportunities to write for a school science magazine, start a blog, or create a YouTube channel about chemistry topics. This is a great way to deepen your understanding.
    • Local Organizations
    • Check with local hospitals, museums, chemical companies, or conservation charities for volunteer opportunities. Many of these places have programs for young people.
