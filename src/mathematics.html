Mathematics

  • Maths Genie - Incredibly popular free resource with topic-by-topic questions, practice papers, and video solutions
  • Corbettmaths - Excellent free resource with vast library of videos, worksheets, and 5-a-day practice questions
  • Dr Frost Maths - Comprehensive free platform with thousands of videos and practice questions used by many schools
  • BBC Bitesize (GCSE & A-Level Maths) - Clear revision notes, video explanations, and interactive activities
  • Physics & Maths Tutor Education - Indispensable free resource with vast revision notes and past papers
  • Exam Solutions - Incredibly valuable free resource with extensive video tutorials covering almost every A-Level topic
  • Integral Maths - High-quality paid resource developed by MEI with comprehensive notes and exercises
  • Khan Academy Mathematics - Wide range of free video lessons and practice exercises for core mathematical principles
  • A Short History of Nearly Everything
  • Bill Bryson
  • While not exclusively mathematics, this book includes numerous fascinating insights into the mathematical discoveries that underpin our understanding of the universe. It's incredibly accessible and engaging for all levels.
  • Fermat's Last Theorem
  • Simon Singh
  • A thrilling detective story about the 350-year quest to prove Fermat's Last Theorem. Singh masterfully explains complex mathematical concepts in an accessible way, making the struggle and triumph of mathematicians palpable. Excellent for both GCSE and A-Level.
  • The Code Book: The Science of Secrecy from Ancient Egypt to Quantum Cryptography
  • Simon Singh
  • As mentioned for Computer Science, this book brilliantly blends history, mathematics, and computer science through the lens of cryptography. It showcases the practical application of number theory and logic.
  • The Joy of X: A Guided Tour of Math, from One to Infinity
  • Steven Strogatz
  • A delightful and highly readable exploration of fundamental mathematical concepts, from basic arithmetic to calculus and beyond, presented with clarity and enthusiasm. Great for building intuition and appreciation.
  • Alex's Adventures in Numberland
  • Alex Bellos
  • A fun and quirky journey through the world of numbers, exploring various mathematical curiosities, puzzles, and surprising applications. Highly engaging for GCSE students.
  • How Not to Be Wrong: The Power of Mathematical Thinking
  • Jordan Ellenberg
  • Explores how mathematical thinking can be applied to everyday life and various fields, helping readers make sense of statistics, probability, and common fallacies. Very relevant for A-Level students developing critical thinking.
  • Alan Turing: The Enigma
  • Andrew Hodges
  • The definitive biography of Alan Turing, a foundational figure in computer science and mathematics. It delves into his work on computation, logic, and his crucial role in code-breaking, showcasing the human side of mathematical genius.
  • The Man Who Knew Infinity: A Life of the Genius Ramanujan
  • Robert Kanigel
  • The inspiring and tragic story of Srinivasa Ramanujan, an Indian mathematical prodigy whose intuitive genius led to profound discoveries despite a lack of formal training. Highlights the beauty of pure mathematics.
  • Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!
  • Richard Feynman
  • While a physicist, Feynman's anecdotes often touch upon his incredible problem-solving skills and unique way of thinking, which is deeply mathematical. An inspiring read for anyone pursuing STEM.
  • Euclid's Window: The Story of Geometry from Parallel Lines to Hyperspace
  • Leonard Mlodinow
  • A historical and conceptual journey through geometry, from Euclidean foundations to non-Euclidean geometries and higher dimensions. Good for those who enjoy spatial reasoning.
  • Logicomix: An Epic Search for Truth
  • Apostolos Doxiadis and Christos Papadimitriou
  • A graphic novel that tells the story of the turbulent early 20th century, when mathematicians grappled with the foundations of logic and mathematics. Features figures like Russell, Godel, and Turing.
  • What is Mathematics? An Elementary Approach to Ideas and Methods
  • Richard Courant and Herbert Robbins
  • A classic text that introduces fundamental mathematical ideas and methods in an accessible way, covering numbers, geometry, topology, and more. A more challenging but very rewarding read for keen A-Level students.
  • The Lady Tasting Tea: How Statistics Revolutionized Science in the Twentieth Century
  • David Salsburg
  • Explores the history and development of modern statistics through engaging stories and key figures. Excellent for understanding the application of probability and data.
  • The Pleasures of Counting
  • T.W. Körner
  • A collection of essays on various mathematical topics, often starting from simple questions and leading to surprising and deep results. Encourages a playful approach to problem-solving.
  • Mathematical Puzzles of Sam Loyd / Mathematical Puzzles of Henry Ernest Dudeney
  • Sam Loyd / Henry Ernest Dudeney
  • Collections of classic mathematical puzzles that encourage logical thinking and problem-solving skills, often requiring clever insights rather than advanced mathematics. Great for all levels.
  • Lumiere Research Scholar Program
  • Apply Here
  • This program pairs high school students with PhD mentors from top universities to conduct independent research projects. They have a specific focus on mathematics and applied sciences.
  • Polygence
  • Visit Website
  • Similar to Lumiere, Polygence connects students with academic mentors for one-on-one research projects. The platform is flexible and can accommodate a wide range of mathematics topics.
  • Cambridge Centre for International Research
  • Visit Website
  • CCIR offers research mentorship programs in various subjects, including mathematics. Their programs are designed to provide a university-level research experience.
  • Immerse Education Mathematics Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • Immerse offers in-person mathematics programs at prestigious universities like Oxford and Cambridge. The curriculum covers pure mathematics, applied mathematics, and statistics.
  • Imperial College London Global Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • This program offers specialized areas of study, including mathematics and engineering, allowing students to engage in academic lectures and practical challenges.
  • Conrad Challenge
  • Visit Website
  • This is a global competition for students to create innovative solutions to global challenges. A team could develop a mathematics-related project to address data analysis or optimization problems.
  • International Mathematical Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This is the most prestigious mathematics competition for high school students globally. To participate, students must typically first succeed in their national mathematics olympiad.
  • Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair
  • Visit Website
  • ISEF is the world's largest pre-college STEM competition. Students qualify through local and regional fairs, and the event offers significant awards and networking opportunities.
  • British Mathematical Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This competition recognizes outstanding high school students for their mathematics knowledge and problem-solving skills. It offers recognition and opportunities for further competition.
  • Online Courses
  • Many universities and platforms offer free or low-cost online courses (MOOCs) in mathematics and related fields. Platforms like Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn have courses from top institutions.
  • Science Communication
  • Look for opportunities to write for a school science magazine, start a blog, or create a YouTube channel about mathematics topics. This is a great way to deepen your understanding.
  • Local Organizations
  • Check with local universities, research institutions, financial companies, or technology firms for volunteer opportunities. Many of these places have programs for young people.
