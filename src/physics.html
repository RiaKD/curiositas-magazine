Physics

  • BBC Bitesize (GCSE & A-Level Physics) - Superb free resource with interactive notes, videos, quizzes, and activities
  • Physics & Maths Tutor Education - Essential free resource with extensive revision notes, past papers, and mark schemes
  • Seneca Learning - Free engaging platform using neuroscience-backed learning methods with GIFs and animations
  • Save My Exams - High-quality revision notes, exam-style questions, and past papers for all exam boards
  • A-Level Physics Online - Comprehensive free video tutorials covering entire AQA specification
  • Isaac Physics - Free platform from University of Cambridge with challenging physics problems
  • Hyperphysics - Fantastic in-depth resource for understanding physics concepts
  • Khan Academy Physics - Wide range of free video lessons and practice exercises for conceptual understanding
  • A Short History of Nearly Everything
  • Bill Bryson
  • As with biology and chemistry, this book offers an incredibly engaging and accessible journey through scientific discoveries, including many fundamental physics concepts, making complex ideas digestible and fun. Highly recommended for both GCSE and A-Level.
  • Cosmos
  • Carl Sagan
  • A classic that beautifully explores the universe, our place in it, and the history of scientific discovery. Sagan's prose is both poetic and clear, making complex astronomical and physical concepts accessible.
  • Astrophysics for People in a Hurry
  • Neil deGrasse Tyson
  • A concise and enthusiastic guide to fundamental concepts in astrophysics, from the Big Bang to dark matter. Great for those with limited time but a big interest in space.
  • Seven Brief Lessons on Physics
  • Carlo Rovelli
  • A wonderfully concise and poetic exploration of some of the most profound ideas in modern physics, including general relativity, quantum mechanics, and the nature of time. More challenging but very rewarding for A-Level students.
  • The Elegant Universe: Superstrings, Hidden Dimensions, and the Quest for the Ultimate Theory
  • Brian Greene
  • While delving into cutting-edge theory, Greene does an excellent job of explaining classical physics concepts like relativity in an intuitive way before moving into more complex ideas. Primarily for A-Level.
  • Surely You're Joking, Mr. Feynman!
  • Richard Feynman
  • A collection of anecdotes and reflections from the Nobel Prize-winning physicist. It offers a glimpse into the mind of a brilliant scientist and his unconventional approach to life and physics. Entertaining and inspiring.
  • The Newtonian Revolution
  • I. Bernard Cohen
  • A classic text that explains the context and impact of Newton's work on physics and beyond. More academic but insightful for those keen on history of science.
  • A Brief History of Time
  • Stephen Hawking
  • A seminal work that attempts to explain complex cosmological concepts, including black holes, the Big Bang, and quantum mechanics, to a general audience. Can be challenging but highly rewarding for A-Level students.
  • Quantum Enigma: Physics Encounters Consciousness
  • Bruce Rosenblum and Fred Kuttner
  • Explores the stranger aspects of quantum mechanics and its philosophical implications, including the measurement problem and the role of the observer. For more advanced A-Level students.
  • Reality Is Not What It Seems: The Journey to Quantum Gravity
  • Carlo Rovelli
  • Continues from "Seven Brief Lessons," delving deeper into quantum gravity and the attempts to unify general relativity and quantum mechanics. Challenging but fascinating for the highly motivated.
  • Black Holes and Baby Universes and Other Essays
  • Stephen Hawking
  • A collection of essays on various topics in cosmology, including black holes, the origin of the universe, and the nature of time.
  • The Fabric of the Cosmos: Space, Time, and the Texture of Reality
  • Brian Greene
  • A comprehensive exploration of the fundamental nature of reality, delving into space, time, and the universe's structure. Longer and more detailed than "The Elegant Universe."
  • Death by Black Hole: And Other Cosmic Quandaries
  • Neil deGrasse Tyson
  • Another engaging collection of essays by Tyson, addressing various intriguing questions about the cosmos with his characteristic enthusiasm.
  • Bad Science
  • Ben Goldacre
  • While not exclusively physics, this book is crucial for developing critical thinking skills by exposing scientific misinformation and promoting evidence-based reasoning. Highly recommended for all STEM students.
  • Lumiere Research Scholar Program
  • Apply Here
  • This program pairs high school students with PhD mentors from top universities to conduct independent research projects. They have a specific focus on physics and engineering.
  • Polygence
  • Visit Website
  • Similar to Lumiere, Polygence connects students with academic mentors for one-on-one research projects. The platform is flexible and can accommodate a wide range of physics topics.
  • Cambridge Centre for International Research
  • Visit Website
  • CCIR offers research mentorship programs in various subjects, including physics. Their programs are designed to provide a university-level research experience.
  • Immerse Education Physics Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • Immerse offers in-person physics programs at prestigious universities like Oxford and Cambridge. The curriculum covers mechanics, thermodynamics, and modern physics.
  • Imperial College London Global Summer School
  • Visit Website
  • This program offers specialized areas of study, including physics and engineering, allowing students to engage in academic lectures and practical challenges.
  • Conrad Challenge
  • Visit Website
  • This is a global competition for students to create innovative solutions to global challenges. A team could develop a physics-related project to address energy or transportation issues.
  • International Physics Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This is the most prestigious physics competition for high school students globally. To participate, students must typically first succeed in their national physics olympiad.
  • Regeneron International Science and Engineering Fair
  • Visit Website
  • ISEF is the world's largest pre-college STEM competition. Students qualify through local and regional fairs, and the event offers significant awards and networking opportunities.
  • British Physics Olympiad
  • Visit Website
  • This competition recognizes outstanding high school students for their physics knowledge and problem-solving skills. It offers recognition and opportunities for further competition.
  • Online Courses
  • Many universities and platforms offer free or low-cost online courses (MOOCs) in physics and related fields. Platforms like Coursera, edX, and FutureLearn have courses from top institutions.
  • Science Communication
  • Look for opportunities to write for a school science magazine, start a blog, or create a YouTube channel about physics topics. This is a great way to deepen your understanding.
  • Local Organizations
  • Check with local science museums, planetariums, engineering companies, or research institutions for volunteer opportunities. Many of these places have programs for young people.
